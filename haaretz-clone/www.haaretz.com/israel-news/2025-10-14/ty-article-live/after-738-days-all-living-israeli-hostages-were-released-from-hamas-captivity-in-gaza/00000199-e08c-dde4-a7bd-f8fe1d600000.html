Report: The U.S.-backed GHF will likely not continue operations in post-war Gaza

A U.S.-backed group that had controlled aid distribution in the Gaza Strip over the past few months will likely cease operations, The Times of Israel reported late Tuesday, citing an unpublished annex to the cease-fire agreement.

"UN agencies, other organizations, the Red Crescent and countries operating in the Strip will receive and distribute aid," the document reportedly reads.

The Gaza Humanitarian Foundation, or GHF, received support from the U.S. and Israel to lead aid efforts in the Strip, despite unclear sources of funding, a lack of experience, and after operations began reports of mass casualties at aid site areas.

Last week, U.S. Ambassador to Israel Mike Huckabee told Haaretz that he expected its operations to continue and denied reports of mass killings at and around their sites.

'He felt it could be done': Trump says Charlie Kirk believed he'd secure Middle East peace

U.S. President Trump's visit to the Middle East was "very historic" as he had secured peace in the region, the president said Tuesday at a ceremony honoring Charlie Kirk, the far-right activist who was murdered last month.

"People said that couldn't be done, but Charlie felt it could be done," the president said.

'No crisis' between Israel and Hamas despite hostages' bodies' late return, diplomat says

A foreign diplomat involved in the Israel-Gaza cease-fire talks told Haaretz that currently there is "no crisis" between Israel and Hamas regarding hostages' bodies.

"Israel took into account that there would be difficulty in locating some of the bodies under the rubble when it signed the agreement," he said.

Drafts of the agreement and leaders had said all hostages, living or dead, would be released within the first 72 hours of the cease-fire. However, as of Tuesday night, only eight bodies out of 28 are likely in Israeli custody, with four currently positively identified as hostages and four unidentified.

Israel's Smotrich vows 'there will be Jewish settlement in Gaza' despite cease-fire deal

Far-right Finance Minister Bezalel Smotrich vowed on Tuesday night that "there will be Jewish settlement in Gaza," despite a cease-fire deal and a U.S.-brokered agreement that does not include such plans.

At an event celebrating the Jewish holiday of Simchat Torah in southern Israel's Sderot, near the Gaza Strip border, Smotrich said, "There will be no Hamas in Gaza, no threat to Israeli civilians from Gaza for decades. There will be Jewish settlement in Gaza, because without long-term settlement, there is no security."

He added, "We have patience, determination, and faith, and with God's help we will continue the series of victories and miracles God has done with us, and achieve a full victory here as well a victorious winter of settlement and security for Sderot."

As he spoke, the crowd chanted "Gush Katif," referring to a group of Jewish settlements dismantled during Israel's 2005 disengagement from Gaza.

RECAP: Four hostages returned on Monday named; Four additional coffins of deceased hostages en route to IDF forces

Here are the latest updates on the release of the hostages:

The Israeli army said it has identified the bodies of Guy Ilouz, Bipin Joshi, Daniel Peretz and Yossi Sharabi, who were returned to Israel on Monday.

The IDF said the Red Cross received four additional coffins of deceased hostages, and that the organization is on its way to Israeli forces inside Gaza.

U.S. President Donald Trump said that he spoke with Hamas and told them they must disarm, and that the group "will disarm because they said they're gonna disarm." Trump also posted on Truth Social, writing that the job of returning all Israeli hostages from Gaza is "NOT DONE," and announced that "Phase Two begins right NOW!!!"

Israel has decided to keep the Rafah crossing closed and to reduce humanitarian aid to the Gaza Strip until Hamas returns the bodies of the slain hostages, following assessments by security officials who said the IDF has not identified any significant efforts by Hamas to locate the bodies.

Israel has told the United Nations it will only allow 300 aid trucks half the agreed number into the Gaza Strip from Wednesday and that no fuel or gas will be allowed into the enclave except for specific needs related to humanitarian infrastructure, according to a note seen by Reuters and confirmed by the UN.

Israeli fire killed six Palestinians in Gaza and wounded another 29 people over the past day, the local Health Ministry reported.

Egyptian teams are operating in Gaza to locate and recover the remains of Israeli hostages, Qatari outlet Al Araby reported.

A first group of bodies of Palestinians killed during the Gaza war has arrived in the enclave after being released by Israel.

Israeli settlers attacked a bulldozer and a vehicle belonging to Palestinians who were operating in the central West Bank with a permit by the Israeli military, Palestinian sources said.

The Hostages and Missing Families Forum announced it will hold an emergency meeting on Tuesday evening due to Hamas' violation of the terms of the cease-fire, which stipulated that all deceased hostages would be released by Monday.

For the first time in more than two years, no living Israeli hostages remain in Hamas captivity. The last 20 living hostages were released from Gaza, after 738 days in captivity, under the cease-fire agreement between Israel and Hamas.
